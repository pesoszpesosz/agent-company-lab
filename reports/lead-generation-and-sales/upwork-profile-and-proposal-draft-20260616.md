## Proposal Template

Subject:

`Workflow audit for your recurring reporting process`

Proposal body:

Hi,

I can help map the reporting workflow and identify where automation can safely reduce manual copy-paste without removing the human review step.

For a first pass, I would focus on:

1. Current workflow map: who sends what, where the data lives, and where delays/errors happen.
2. Automation candidates: imports, validation checks, report templates, summary drafts, approval logs.
3. Risk controls: where a person should approve text, numbers, client-specific language, or final delivery.
4. A small synthetic proof asset so you can see the shape before any private data is used.

I recently drafted a sample audit for a synthetic marketing agency workflow: weekly client reporting took about 4 hours per client and had copy-paste, naming, and approval-trail risks. The proposed low-risk architecture used spreadsheet validation, template-based summaries, and a human review queue before final delivery.

I would not need credentials or private client data for the initial audit. Any live integration, real data, account access, or automation that sends client-facing material should be scoped separately and approved before implementation.

Thanks.
