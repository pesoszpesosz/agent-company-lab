## Offer Being Proved

Offer name: Website Intake and Missed-Lead Audit

Plain-language offer:

Review a small business website's public intake path and produce a concise improvement plan for visitors who want a quote, appointment, consultation, or service request.

What the offer can include:

- One-page intake-path summary.
- Pass/fail rubric results.
- Screenshots or notes from allowed public pages when a future service request permits real-source review.
- Specific fixes that can be done without account takeover or sensitive customer data.
- A simple implementation scope, such as clarifying a call to action, reducing form friction, adding a service-area note, or improving confirmation copy.

What the offer must not claim:

- Guaranteed revenue, ranking, response-rate, conversion-rate, compliance, or savings.
- Security, legal, medical, financial, tax, insurance, or regulated advice.
- Access to private analytics, inboxes, CRMs, ads accounts, payment systems, or customer data.
- Permission to contact anyone.

## Rubric Scoring Model

Use this rubric to demonstrate the shape of a future proof artifact. In a real-lead phase, every checked page/source must first pass the source-rights and service-request gates. For now, all rows below are template criteria only.

Result values:

- `pass`: The page gives a typical visitor enough clarity to continue.
- `fail`: The page creates obvious friction or missing information.
- `needs_review`: The item may be acceptable, but it needs human judgment or a scoped follow-up.
- `not_applicable`: The item does not apply to the fictional business or future target type.

| # | Check | Pass Criteria | Fail Criteria | Evidence Note Required |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Service clarity | Visitor can tell what service is offered within the first screen. | Service category is vague, buried, or hidden behind generic slogans. | Quote the page section or note the missing label. |
| 2 | Primary call to action | One main action is visible: request quote, book, call, or contact. | Multiple competing CTAs, no CTA, or CTA appears only after long scrolling. | Record CTA text and location. |
| 3 | Contact path | Visitor can reach a business inquiry path in one to two clicks/taps. | Contact path is missing, broken, hidden, or split across conflicting pages. | Record route from homepage to contact path. |
| 4 | Intake form friction | Form asks only for fields needed to start a conversation. | Form demands excessive, sensitive, confusing, or duplicate fields. | List fields and mark optional/required if visible. |
| 5 | Mobile usability | CTA and contact path are usable on a typical mobile viewport. | CTA is obscured, too small, off-screen, or hard to tap. | Note viewport and issue without collecting real users. |
| 6 | Response expectation | Site sets a basic expectation for what happens after inquiry. | No confirmation, timeline, service area, or next-step expectation. | Record visible promise or missing next step. |
| 7 | Service area fit | Visitor can tell whether the business serves their location. | Service area is absent, ambiguous, or contradictory. | Note stated area or absence. |
| 8 | Trust signal | Site shows at least one relevant trust signal: license note, reviews link, portfolio, guarantee limits, years in business, or insurance statement. | No credibility signals or claims that look unsupported. | Record signal type, not personal reviewer data. |
| 9 | Privacy/data minimization | Site avoids asking for sensitive data before a clear need. | Site requests sensitive data too early or without explanation. | List sensitive fields if present. |
| 10 | Accessibility basics | Text contrast, labels, and button purpose appear understandable from normal inspection. | Obvious unlabeled form controls, unreadable text, or icon-only critical actions. | Note observable issue only. |
| 11 | Broken-flow check | Primary links and forms appear reachable in allowed read-only review. | Broken links, dead buttons, unclear submit state, or contradictory destinations. | Record path and observed breakage. |
| 12 | Offer fit | A small audit/fix would plausibly help without private access. | Fix would require private data, account takeover, regulated advice, or major rebuild. | State why fit is safe or unsafe. |

Suggested scoring:

- 10 to 12 pass: strong intake path; no outreach need unless there is a very specific fit trigger.
- 7 to 9 pass: possible fit if failures are narrow and fixable.
- 4 to 6 pass: possible fit, but avoid outreach until proof and ethics review confirm the issue is not exploitative.
- 0 to 3 pass: do not pursue without a separate human review; high risk of low-quality or irrelevant outreach.

## Review Gates Before Any Real Use

No real source review, lead list, contact collection, CRM upload, or outreach action can occur unless an approved service request names the exact scope. The service request must answer:

| Gate | Required Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Lane | Must be `lead_generation_and_sales`. |

| Data fields | Minimum fields to collect, with no personal/sensitive data unless separately approved. |
| Jurisdiction check | CAN-SPAM/TCPA/privacy review appropriate to the proposed channel and target. |
| Suppression | Do-not-contact and opt-out handling exists before any send. |

| Rate limit | Tiny pilot limit and stop conditions. |
| Artifact path | Local proof artifact path and trace/outcome plan. |
| Approval owner | Explicit approver and exact approved scope. |

Default decision: if any gate is incomplete, do not proceed.
